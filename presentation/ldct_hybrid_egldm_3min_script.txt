Good evening, everyone. I'm Zhexi, and today we’re discussing a critical trade-off in medical imaging: how to reduce radiation in CT scans without sacrificing diagnostic integrity.

Slide 1:

The main challenge with Low-Dose CT denoising is 'hallucination.' While modern diffusion models are powerful, they risk creating anatomical structures that aren't actually there.

Our solution is a Conservative Refiner. Instead of letting diffusion generate the image from scratch, we use it as a targeted refiner built around a stable, deterministic RED-CNN anchor. This ensures that every pixel remains tethered to reality.

Slide 2:

To make this claim credible, the protocol is everything. We used the LIDC-IDRI dataset, curating over 243,000 valid slices from 1,010 patients.

Most importantly, we enforced a strict patient-level split. By synthesizing low-dose observations through a signal-dependent Gaussian model on raw DICOM data, we ensure our evidence is entirely leakage-free.
